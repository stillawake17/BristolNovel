Introduction

Welcome to the Bristol Novel Criteria site. Here we explore the characteristics that make a novel truly Bristol.

Criteria for Judging How "Bristol" a Novel Is

Below are the criteria to determine how intrinsically linked a novel is to the essence of Bristol:

1. Historical Significance

  • Representation of Bristol's History: Does the novel showcase notable historical events or periods significant to Bristol?
  • Character Historical Depth: Are the characters rooted in Bristol's history? Do they have backstories intertwined with the city's past?

2. Geographical Accuracy and Depth

  • Landmarks: Does the novel mention or revolve around iconic Bristol landmarks like the Clifton Suspension Bridge or the SS Great Britain?
  • City Layout: Is the geographical layout of Bristol accurately portrayed?
  • Wards: Are specific Bristol wards (like Clifton, Redland, etc.) mentioned or described?

3. Cultural Representation

  • Events: Does the novel include Bristol-specific events, like the Bristol Balloon Fiesta or the Harbour Festival?
  • Local Lingo: Are Bristolian phrases or slang used by the characters?
  • Arts & Music: Does it feature Bristol's arts and music scenes?

4. Social and Contemporary Issues

  • Current Events: Does the novel touch upon or delve into contemporary issues Bristol faces, like housing crises or environmental challenges?
  • Historical Context: Does it address significant events, like the toppling of the statue of slave trader Edward Colston?

5. Overall "Feel"

  • Authenticity: Does the novel capture the unique "feel" of Bristol? Does it represent the spirit and essence of the city?

6. Local Media Representation

  • Media Outlets: Are Bristol-based media outlets like "Bristol Post", "Bristol 24/7", or "BBC Radio Bristol" referenced or featured in the novel?
  • Media Personalities: Does the novel mention or include characters inspired by well-known local journalists, radio hosts, or TV personalities?
  • Media Events: Are there references to events or stories that made headlines in Bristol's local news?
  • Media's Role: Does the novel explore the role of local media in shaping public opinion, covering events, or interacting with the community?
